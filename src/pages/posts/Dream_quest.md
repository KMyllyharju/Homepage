My psychoanalyst Dr. Carlisle tells me that dreams are windows into the subconscious mind. The meaning of dreams is not literal, but rather comes through association. He suggests that the beacon of light that is oft present in my dreams represents a desire to find meaning and direction in my life after the passing of my beloved wife. He suggests that the ever-present winged serpents are symbols of our unborn child who perished with her. A symbol of deceit; luring me with promise of kin, but reneging on the deal and taking my wife as further insult.

I do not resent her or my child, I merely resent the world for making me believe that it was my right as a man to see my own flesh and blood grow up and enjoy sharing my life with someone who sees good in me even when I cannot see it in myself. My wife was like that. Oh, what I would give for her to rescue me once more from the dark pits of my own mind.

I see her sometimes, in my dreams. In the dream she seems as she was in life but when I wake I realize that she is more perfect in my dream than she ever was during the day. It is comforting in a way to know that within my subconscious I see her as an idealized version of herself. Oh, if only I could tell her of how she is in my dreams. She is like a radiant angel made of pure light and love, without a single blemish in her figure or an unkind word on her lips.

I find myself sleeping more and more. Perhaps it is all in order to see her again and again. Even my days go by in a tired haze, and the line between dreams and reality begins to blur. Even when I know I am awake, I see glances of those serpents in the corner of my eye, but they disappear as soon as I try to look upon them directly. I fear to tell Dr. Carlisle about this.

The landscapes of my dreams change but the beacon of light in the distance always remains. In most of my dreams I journey towards that light, but I am never sure of what I will find at the end of my quest. Sometimes the light is thin and far in the distance, other times it is thick and nearby, yet it is never reached. Dr. Carlisle suggests that simply having a goal is more important than the end result. That goals drive us forward in life, and make it worth living. He suggests this is all a representation of my subconscious quest for a quest; that finding a goal is my goal.

---

I awake once more, within a dream. This time I am almost engulfed in light. I can feel that the beacon is closer than it has ever been before. How I know this, I cannot tell, but I am certain of it. 

I find myself amongst an ocean where winds have taken the role of waves. I stand upon the sandy beach of an isle, hovering above an endless expanse of pale blue sky. The waves of wind lap the sands of the beach gently like the touch of a lover, causing the edge of the beach to sway back and forth in a perfectly choreographed dance. The entire beach is bathed in a pale white light that feels warm and comforting. Without even thinking about it I know that this light is the light of my beacon, beckoning me closer. It shines bright from a single spot near the horizon, snaking past objects, forming only faint and twisted shadows.

Directly in front of me floats a large island consisting of two city blocks that appear to have been torn from the ground. On the edge of the isle I see broken water pipes within dirt that lay under a layer of cracked concrete. Did an entire city lie somewhere far beneath my feet, that served as the source of these islands? Perhaps it is a city like Atlantis, hidden beneath clouds instead of water. I can feel the light drawing me towards the island, but cannot find a way on to it. It floats so close that I feel I can almost touch it, yet far enough that I know I can not jump the gap.

I am suddenly alerted to the presence of an incredibly tall man standing right beside me, in total silence. He is monstrously tall, taller than any human could be and his skin is black like the cosmos. Yet despite his ebony complexion, his facial features do not seem typical for those of the dark continent, instead appearing to be rather Caucasian. I do not know when or from where he has emerged, but I also do not find his sudden appearance to be alarming. I do not ever recall seeing him or any other people in my dreams, yet in the moment he seems as natural of a part of this world as the flying serpents and the floating islands.

Without uttering a word, the giant raises his hand and as he does, from the depths below rise a thin bridge of flat stones. These stones connect the beach we stand on to the island ahead. He lowers his hand and nods toward me approvingly. Is it his way of signaling his approval of my quest? Or simply a sign that the bridge is now safe to walk on? Despite not knowing exactly what I am responding to, I nod back in an effort to communicate my gratitude to the man, but I am unsure if he understands. He had allowed me to get closer than ever to my beacon of light, for which I owe him an immense debt of gratitude. Dr. Carlisle often speaks of symbols within dreams. I wonder, what might he say this man symbolizes?

---

The new island is much larger than it initially seemed. I walk for tens of minutes through several near identical blocks of 5 story, gray, brick buildings. Yet, I have no concerns of getting lost in this labyrinth of mirrors, as my compass of the soul remains firmly fixed on the beacon. 

At last, I emerge into an oasis amongst this desert of gray. Hidden in an intersection of buildings lays a small patch of perfect green tranquility. A small, perfectly circular patch of grass, sprouting orchids and violets. On the edge, bringing shade, is a small apple tree - no taller than 3 feet - filled with the most picturesque red apples. In its shade lays a small, square wooden pedestal, holding aloft a glowing, white orb.

I approach this small patch of heaven with haste, filled with dread that I might awake at any moment and never be able to reach the orb again. As I approach, I note that the apple tree is inhabited by a single winged serpent that follows me with its gaze. 

Upon reaching the orb, I instinctively grab it with both arms into my embrace. The orb is warm like the sun on an early summer day. The light it emanates is not blinding, but rather pleasantly illuminating and I have no trouble gazing at it directly. Clutching the orb fills me with a sense of warmth and fulfillment that I had not felt for a long time. The snake in the tree gazes at me with a look that I think may be one of approval. I can feel the light break though the darkness of my mind and allow me to think clearly again. For the first time in a long time my mind feels clear. This must be a sign that I have to move on and find new light in the waking world. I can now see that my wife would abhor how I have allowed myself to turn into a husk of the man she loved. She would detest how I have been unable to love and forgive myself as she would have loved and forgiven me. I can almost hear her whisper: “The future is too short to be spent thinking only of the past”. This dream has allowed me to find the forgiveness I needed within the visages of my subconscious mind.

However all of this was proven to be false when I awoke - still clutching the orb.
